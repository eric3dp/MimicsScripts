filter_sharp_triangles(entities, filter_distance=0.01, threshold_angle=5.0, filter_mode=<FilterSharpTriangleMode.Collapse: 'Collapse'>)[source]¶

Removes sharp, thin and long triangles of the selected parts.

Parameters:
  • entities (trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet | [trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet]) – Either a single object or heterogeneous list of parts, surfaces, or surface sets.
  • filter_distance (float, optional) – Triangles with an edge or height smaller than this distance will be filtered.
  • threshold_angle (float, optional) – A triangle will be filtered when the angle it makes with its neighbors is larger than the threshold angle.
  • filter_mode (FilterSharpTriangleMode, optional) – 2 Options - Collapse and Delete. With Collapse, the thin triangle will be removed and its neighbours will be connected to each other. With Delete, the thin triangle will be deleted, and bad edges will appear.
Returns:

Fixed input entities.
Return type:

trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet | (trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet)
Raises:

ValueError, RuntimeError

reduce(entities, flip_threshold_angle=15.0, geometrical_error=0.05, number_of_iterations=5, preserve_surfaces=False)[source]¶

This function reduces the number of triangles in a chosen entity. The number of triangles will be as small as possible within given geometric error constraints.

Parameters:
  • entities (trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet | trimatic.MarkedTriangles | [trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet | trimatic.MarkedTriangles]) – Either single object or heterogeneous list of parts, surfaces, or surface sets.
  • flip_threshold_angle (float, optional) – This parameter avoids that triangles will flip during the reduce process. It determines the maximum allowed deviation between the normals of the triangles before and after the reduction.
  • geometrical_error (float, optional) – Maximum error that can be produced by the reduction.
  • number_of_iterations (int, optional) – Higher number of iterations will further reduce the entity’s number of triangles.
  • preserve_surfaces (bool, optional) – If True, the geometry of surface contours will be preserved during reduction. This is useful when there are surfaces defined that are not based on the part geometry.
Returns:

Reduced parts
Return type:

trimatic.Part | trimatic.Surface | trimatic.SurfaceSet | (trimatic.Part) | (trimatic.Surface) | (trimatic.SurfaceSet)
Raises:

ValueError, RuntimeError
Example:

import os

                    number_of_iterations=5,preserve_surfaces=False)
